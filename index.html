Discover Arizona's Best Trails

Welcome to Desert Trails AZ, your go-to resource for hiking trails in and around the Phoenix and Tempe, Arizona area. Whether you are a first-time hiker or a seasoned outdoor enthusiast, we have something for everyone. Explore stunning desert landscapes, rocky mountain summits, and hidden canyon gems right in your backyard.

Why Hike in Arizona?

Arizona is home to some of the most breathtaking and diverse terrain in the United States. From the towering saguaro cacti of the Sonoran Desert to the red rock formations of Sedona, every trail offers a unique and unforgettable experience. With over 300 days of sunshine per year, Arizona is a hiker's paradise almost year-round.

  • Miles of scenic trails for all skill levels
  • Stunning desert sunrises and sunsets
  • Diverse wildlife including roadrunners, javelinas, and eagles
  • Accessible trails close to the Phoenix metro area

Featured Trails

Not sure where to start? Here are three of our most recommended trails for visitors and newcomers to Arizona hiking:

Camelback Mountain

Difficulty: Hard

Distance: 5.5 miles

Location: Phoenix, AZ

One of the most iconic hikes in Arizona. The summit offers panoramic views of the entire Phoenix valley. Best tackled in cooler months.

South Mountain Park

Difficulty: Easy

Distance: 2.5 miles

Location: Phoenix, AZ

A great beginner-friendly option close to Tempe. Wide, well-marked trails with beautiful desert scenery and petroglyphs along the way.

Fremont Saddle

Difficulty: Moderate

Distance: 5.8 miles

Location: Superstition Mountains, AZ

A classic Arizona hike that rewards you with stunning views of Weaver's Needle. Drive the scenic Apache Trail to get there.

Plan Your Hike

Before you hit the trail, make sure you are prepared. Arizona's desert climate can be unpredictable, especially during summer months when temperatures regularly exceed 110 degrees. Visit our Tips & Gear page for essential advice on staying safe and having a great experience on the trail.

For more information on Arizona's state parks and outdoor recreation, visit the official Arizona State Parks website.
