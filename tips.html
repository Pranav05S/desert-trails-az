Tips & Gear for Arizona Hiking

Everything you need to know before hitting the trail

Hiking in Arizona is an incredible experience, but the desert environment requires special preparation. Whether you are heading out for a quick morning walk or a full-day adventure, following these tips from Desert Trails AZ will help keep you safe, comfortable, and ready to enjoy every step of the trail.

Essential Safety Tips

Hydration

Water is the most important thing you can bring on any Arizona hike. Drink at least one liter of water per hour in hot weather. Start hydrating before you even reach the trailhead. Never rely on streams or natural water sources in the desert as they are often dry or contaminated.

Timing Your Hike

Always start early — ideally before 7am during summer months. Arizona temperatures can exceed 110°F by midday from May through September. If you are hiking in summer, plan to be back at your car by 10am. Fall and spring offer the most comfortable conditions for longer hikes.

Sun Protection

Apply sunscreen with at least SPF 30 before heading out and reapply every two hours. Wear a wide-brimmed hat and UV-protective sunglasses. Light-colored, long-sleeved clothing can actually keep you cooler than shorts and a t-shirt by reflecting heat and protecting your skin.

Recommended Gear

  • Insulated water bottles or a hydration pack (minimum 2 liters)
  • Sturdy, closed-toe hiking shoes or trail runners
  • Wide-brimmed sun hat and UV-rated sunglasses
  • Sunscreen SPF 30 or higher
  • Trail snacks such as nuts, granola bars, or dried fruit
  • A fully charged phone with a downloaded offline map
  • A small first aid kit with blister treatment
  • A flashlight or headlamp in case your hike runs long

Know Before You Go

Always tell someone where you are hiking and when you expect to return. Check the weather forecast before heading out and be aware of monsoon season from July through September, when sudden storms can create dangerous flash flooding in canyon areas. For the latest trail conditions and safety alerts, visit the Arizona State Parks website.

Trail Etiquette

Good trail etiquette makes hiking better for everyone. Always yield to uphill hikers, keep your dog on a leash, pack out all of your trash, and stay on marked trails to protect the surrounding desert ecosystem. Leave No Trace principles apply to all Arizona trails — take only photos and leave only footprints.
